01 — Thesis & brand position

The non-negotiables.

Goodreads-class in scope and polish — millions of books, real discovery, full feature breadth — built on a different foundation: critical pedagogy, scaffolded reflection, and Yumi as the structuring intelligence. Goodreads gamifies quantity of consumption. This app cultivates depth of becoming.

Hard constraints. Yumi does not summarize books. Star ratings exist but are de-emphasized. No follower counts, like counts, or reshare counts as primary UI. Reviews are pedagogically scaffolded, not free-form. Recommendations driven by engagement depth and concept overlap, not collaborative filtering on ratings. One Book Artifact per user per book — strict structural rule. The Notebook is fully private, always.

06 — Brand position map

What the app is. What it isn't. What other reading tools are.

What this app is

  • A reading community organized by depth of engagement
  • A practice tool: notebook, conversations, scaffolded reflection
  • A publishing surface for one coherent Book Artifact per book
  • A subject-based learning instrument: Learning Arcs across angles
  • A bookselling channel for readers who want curated paths
  • Pedagogically grounded — Freire, hooks, Giroux, De Lissovoy
  • Yumi is the structuring intelligence, not a chatbot bolted on

What this app is not

  • Not Goodreads — not a database of star ratings or short opinions
  • Not BookTok — not engagement-maximizing, not algorithmic feeds
  • Not a journal app — output is shaped, not raw
  • Not a review aggregator — no scores, no rankings, no "top picks"
  • Not a summary generator — Yumi never summarizes
  • Not a follower platform — no follower counts as primary UI
  • Not a quantity tracker — no "30 books in 2026" challenges

How it sits next to other reading tools

ToolWhat it doesHow this app differs
GoodreadsCatalog + ratings + lists + light socialThis app removes ratings as primary signal, replaces with engagement depth.
SubstackLong-form publishing for writers with audiencesThis app is for readers, not writers. Output emerges from reading, not writing for an audience.
Reading journalsPrivate notebooks, no communityThis app keeps the private notebook but adds a public artifact per book, shaped by Yumi.
StoryGraphBetter catalog, mood-based recommendationsThis app builds recommendations from concept overlap, not mood matching.
BookTok / BookstagramVisual culture around books, peer recommendationThis app structurally rejects the engagement-metric mechanics those platforms run on.

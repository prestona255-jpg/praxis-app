# Bookshop.org Setup — Handoff Prompt

*Paste this into a fresh Claude chat when you sit down to apply for Bookshop.org's affiliate program. Apply early — approval can take days.*

---

```
I'm applying for the Bookshop.org affiliate program for a new app I'm building called the Yumi Book App.

CONTEXT:
- The app is a reading community app, philosophically aligned with independent bookstores
- Anti-Amazon brand position — Bookshop.org is the primary, on-brand bookselling channel
- Every public Learning Arc in the app will auto-generate a Bookshop affiliate storefront
- Users can buy a book directly through the app via the affiliate link
- I'm a solo developer; the app is in early build (Phase 1 will have 1-3 testers, expanding from there)
- I'm also pursuing an EdD in education and writing under an author identity (Roland Blair)

WHAT I NEED FROM YOU:
1. Walk me through finding the affiliate application page on Bookshop.org
2. Help me write the application — specifically:
   - A short, honest description of where I'll be promoting books (the Yumi Book App + my content brand)
   - A reasonable estimate of audience size for first year (be realistic; they don't reject for being small)
   - Any pitch language about my values alignment with Bookshop's mission
3. Tell me what to expect after submission (timeline, communication)
4. Once I'm approved (or while I wait), help me think through:
   - What my first storefront should be called
   - Whether to do one master storefront or multiple themed ones from the start
   - The slug for the URL
5. Help me note where to find my affiliate ID once approved (I'll need it for the app's integrations.js)

CONSTRAINTS:
- I want my storefront branding to align with the app's aesthetic (warm, serif-typography, anti-engagement-metric, pedagogy-grounded)
- I want flexibility to create per-arc storefronts later — what does Bookshop's storefront system actually allow?
- I do NOT want to set up Amazon Associates yet — that comes later when the app is more publicly visible

CHECKPOINT BEHAVIOR:
- Walk me through one part at a time
- After each part, confirm with me before proceeding
- If I have a question, pause and answer

OUTPUT I NEED AT THE END:
- A submitted affiliate application
- Notes on what to do during the wait (think through storefronts, draft brand language)
- A plan for what to do once approved (create first storefront, get affiliate ID, document it)

If anything in Bookshop's UI changes from what you expect, ask me to describe what I'm seeing.

Ready when you are. Start with one alignment question — maybe ask about my content brand context so you can help me write the application copy.
```

---

## What to do with this

1. Open a fresh Claude chat (web or desktop)
2. Paste the prompt block above
3. Claude asks one alignment question — answer it
4. Walk through application + post-application planning together
5. At the end you should have:
   - Submitted application
   - A clear post-approval action list
   - Draft language for storefront names/descriptions

## Why apply now even though Stage 8 is months away

Bookshop's affiliate program approval takes 2–7 business days, sometimes longer. If you wait until Stage 8 to apply, you'll hit a stall right when you're trying to integrate the bookselling layer. Applying now means by the time you reach Stage 8, you're already approved and the integration work doesn't block on external approval.

There's also a brand-side reason: a Bookshop affiliate storefront is itself a public surface. Even before the app is live, your storefront could be linked from your content brand (Roland Blair) or from any essay you publish. The earlier the storefront exists, the longer it has to accumulate reach.

## Don't forget

Note your affiliate ID somewhere safe the moment you're approved. You'll need it for the integrations.js bookshop functions in Stage 8, and Bookshop's dashboard isn't the easiest place to dig it up later.
